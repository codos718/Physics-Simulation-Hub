Ohm’s Law

Ohm’s Law describes the relationship between voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit.

Formula:
V = I × R

Meaning:
• Increasing voltage increases current
• Increasing resistance decreases current

Example:
If you connect a 9V battery to a 3Ω resistor, the current will be:
I = V / R = 9V / 3Ω = 3A
