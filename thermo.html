Fission Chain Reaction

About Chain Reactions

This simulation demonstrates how a nuclear chain reaction works, inspired by:

When a neutron collides with a Uranium-235 nucleus, it causes the nucleus to split (fission), releasing fission fragments and more neutrons. If enough nuclei are present, new neutrons keep causing more fissions—a chain reaction!

Key concepts:
  • One event triggers many others (branching)
  • Energy and particles multiply rapidly
  • Visualized here as moving neutrons and fragmenting nuclei
